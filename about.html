US Army

  • Bethesda, Maryland: 2014 - 2019
    • I was stationed in Bethesda, Maryland working at Walter Reed National Military Medical Center (WRNMMC) as a medical laboratory technician (MLT) in the clinical microscopy section in the department of pathology.
    My roles involved training incoming students or new staff members on how to use the instruments and analyzers; running patient specimens, transferring patient results from the instruments to computer software; performing, reviewing, and logging quality control (QC) data performed on the instruments; and sometimes calibrating the instruments.

  • Vilsesck, Germany: 2012 - 2014
    • Serving as a Medical Laboratory Specialist (68K) with the 2nd Calvary Regiment (2CR), Regiment Support Squadron (RSS), I was responsible for all laboratory equipment and supplies. I learned to set up a laboratory environment in tents, if no buildings were available, that were set up in an empty field or terrain.
    During months of soldiers getting ready to deploy or coming back from deployment, I assited in the Soldier Readiness Pre-Deployment and Post-Deployment (SRP) by training army medics in drawing blood (phlebotomy) in a daily basis, supervising the medics, and then personally transporting the specimens in a 2-8 degree Celsius container to the lab for testing.
